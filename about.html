Meet the founder

Built by an agent who was tired of being a data-entry clerk.

LeadCove exists because Julieta Cordero — a working real estate advisor at SERHANT., with prior tenure at Sotheby's, Douglas Elliman, and Side — lived the problem first. Pulling lists, hunting down owner contact info, organizing spreadsheets, all before she could pick up the phone. The dashboard she wished existed didn't, so she built it.

The journey

Julieta Cordero (formerly Julieta Dalmau) is a real estate advisor at SERHANT., with prior tenure at Sotheby's, Douglas Elliman, and Side. Born and raised in South Florida, she attended Tulane University on a tennis scholarship before returning home to build her career. She works the Florida market in English, Spanish, and conversational French — and brings the operating discipline of a competitive athlete to every transaction.
FL LIC# 3542636

What she kept noticing: agents spend more time finding leads than working them. Pulling lists, scrubbing duplicates, hunting down owner phone numbers, copy-pasting into spreadsheets — the prep work consumed the parts of the day that should have been spent on the phone. The tools she tried either didn't deliver clean owner data, charged for misses, or required learning a whole new platform on top of the CRM she already used.

Why LeadCove

The premise was simple: an agent should drop in any list and get back the owner data — name, phone, and/or email — ready to call. No data-entry tax. No paying for rows that came back empty. No starting over with a new CRM.

She built LeadCove with one rule: the platform earns its keep only when it saves the agent time. Misses don't cost a credit. Same owner across multiple properties counts as one. And every record stays in the agent's account — yours, even if you cancel.

Today

LeadCove is open nationwide as of May 1, 2026. Real estate agents from coast to coast can sign up, drop in their first list, and have owner contact data on every match within minutes. The product is built and run by working agents, not a software company guessing at what agents need.
