What does a real estate prospecting credit actually cost?

5 min read · By the LeadCove team

Most prospecting tools price by the seat, by the month, or by some vague "credits per tier" structure that makes it hard to know what a single enriched lead actually costs you. LeadCove publishes the number directly. One credit = one prospect enriched (owner name, verified phone, email, DNC flag, joint-owner data). Add a real dollar figure to that credit, and you have a unit cost you can put in a spreadsheet next to your closed-listing revenue.

This post is the math, fully transparent: what each pack costs, what each credit inside that pack costs, and which configuration fits which use case. No tricks, no hidden auto-renewal, no expiration on what you buy.

The pattern is clean: buy more, pay less per credit. The Trial pack lets you sample at 50 cents a lookup; the Volume pack drops the effective cost to 20 cents per enriched prospect. Same data delivered either way — multi-phone with per-phone DNC pills, joint-owner data, LLC unmasking, email discovery. Pack size only changes the per-credit cost, not the data quality.

How packs work alongside your monthly plan

Every paid LeadCove account starts with a monthly subscription: Starter $29/mo, Pro $49/mo, or Power $99/mo — each with its own included monthly credit allowance (see the pricing page on the main site for the per-tier numbers). Plan credits refresh every billing cycle. They're sized for your typical month.

Packs are for the months that aren't typical. The 12 days you decide to canvass an entire building. The week you pull 400 expireds in a quarterly batch. The investor sprint where you work a whole ZIP for a single off-market acquisition. When you hit your monthly plan limit and still have leads to call, you top up with a pack.

Three things to know about pack credits specifically:

  • No auto-renewal. You buy a pack once. It doesn't recharge automatically. No surprise charge on your card next month.
  • No expiration. Pack credits sit in your account until you spend them. If you buy a Volume pack in May and don't touch it until August, those credits are still there.
  • Stacks on top of your plan. Plan credits get used first each month (they refresh anyway), then pack credits draw down. Maximizes the value of your plan AND your packs.

Real scenarios — when to buy which pack

Scenario 1 — "I'm new and just want to try it"

Sign up for Starter ($49, 100 credits). That's enough to enrich and test the workflow on a real list. If you find yourself working through it in week one, grab a Trial pack ($10, 40 credits) to extend the runway and confirm the math works for your practice before committing to a higher plan tier.

Scenario 2 — "I had an unusually big week"

You're on Pro ($99, 250 credits/month) and you decide to canvass a 300-unit building. Your plan covers 250, you need another 50. Buy a Starter pack ($25, 125 credits) — it covers the overflow and leaves you with 75 unused credits sitting in the account for next time. No expiration, so they're still there in two months when you next need them.

Scenario 3 — "Quarterly expired-listings sprint"

Many agents work expireds in batches — a few hundred at a time, every few months. If you're pulling 600 expireds in one sitting and you're on Pro, your plan covers 250 and you need 350+ more credits. The Pro pack ($99, 550 credits) is the right shape — drops your effective per-credit cost to $0.18 on the overflow and leaves you with cushion for the next batch.

Scenario 4 — "Investor sourcing a market"

Investors working a 1,000-property target zone benefit most from the Volume pack. $249 for 1,750 credits = $0.14 per enriched lead, the lowest unit cost we offer. Combine with any plan tier; packs draw down after plan credits.

What you're actually paying for at $0.14–$0.49 per credit

It's easy to look at the per-credit cost and forget what's delivered for it. Every enriched lead returns:

  • Owner name(s), including the human behind LLCs and trusts.
  • Multiple verified phone numbers (typically 3–5 per owner).
  • Per-phone DNC and TCPA-litigator flags — the compliance signals that prevent $500 to $1,500 TCPA fines per call.
  • Multiple email addresses ranked by likely deliverability.
  • Joint-owner data when a property is co-owned (spouse, partner, family).
  • Property attributes — equity estimate, years held, owner-occupied flag, and more, captured from the source data at no extra charge.

For context, paid lookups with the same depth of data — multi-phone, compliance flags, entity unmasking — typically run between $0.30 and $2.00 per lookup if you go direct to the underlying providers. LeadCove bundles the lookups, runs them through compliance scrubs, adds the dashboard layer, and delivers the result for as little as 14 cents on the Volume pack.

The "no data, no charge" iron rule

One more piece of the math nobody else publishes: if our lookup returns no usable contact data, we don't charge a credit. Ever. So the per-credit cost above is the cost per successful enrichment, not the cost per attempt. Miss rates don't tax your balance.

For a typical Florida residential list, the hit rate runs 85–95%. For LLC-owned commercial property, it's slightly lower. Whatever the hit rate, you pay for hits — the misses are on us.

The simple decision tree: if you regularly burn through your monthly plan, upgrade the plan. If you occasionally hit a busy month, buy a pack and keep the smaller plan. The Volume pack alone, at $0.14 per credit, is the cheapest prospecting data you can buy from any compliance-clean vendor — period.
