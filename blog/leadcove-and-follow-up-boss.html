Integrations

Stop typing leads into Follow Up Boss.

7 min read · By the LeadCove team

Most working agents we talk to already pay for Follow Up Boss. They like the pipeline view, the action plans, the SmartLists, the texting. FUB is a great CRM. It just doesn't enrich.

So agents end up doing one of two things, both bad. Either (1) they hand-type every owner name and phone number into FUB after looking it up on three other websites, or (2) they leave FUB half-empty and dial off a spreadsheet — which defeats the entire point of paying for the CRM.

LeadCove plugs that gap. You bring a list of addresses. We deliver the owner data. It auto-syncs to Follow Up Boss. You keep doing everything you already do in FUB — action plans, SmartLists, follow-up cadences — except now there's actually something in FUB to follow up on.

What FUB is great at — and what it doesn't do

Here's an honest breakdown of where FUB shines and where it leaves you stranded.

What Follow Up Boss is great at

  • Pipeline management. Stages, deal value, expected close date.
  • Action plans + automation. Auto-text on day 0, auto-email on day 3, follow-up reminder on day 7.
  • SmartLists. "Show me everyone in Brickell who I haven't called in 14 days who isn't on Do Not Call." That kind of slicing.
  • Team collaboration. Round-robin assignments, ownership rules, deal credit splits.
  • Two-way text + email. Conversations live in the contact record, not your phone.

What Follow Up Boss does not do

  • Source leads. FUB assumes you've already got contacts.
  • Enrich addresses. Drop in a property, FUB doesn't know who owns it.
  • Surface owner phone or email. Same gap — you have to find that data somewhere else.
  • Unmask LLC / Trust / Corp owners. The exact properties that often have the most equity.
  • Flag DNC and litigators. FUB stores the phone, doesn't tell you whether you can legally call it.

Notice the pattern: FUB manages contacts beautifully once they exist. Everything before that — the part that turns a list of addresses into a contact worth managing — is on you.

The setup (literally four steps)

One-time wiring. Five minutes start to finish, including the coffee break.

  1. In Follow Up Boss, go to Admin → API → Create API Key. Name it "LeadCove" and copy the key.
  2. In LeadCove, open Settings → Integrations → Follow Up Boss.
  3. Paste the key. Click Connect. We test the credential and confirm.
  4. That's it. Every lead you enrich from now on auto-pushes to FUB the moment owner data resolves.

No webhooks to configure, no Zapier, no developer time. The integration is bidirectional: LeadCove rows deep-link to the FUB person record (one-click open from your dashboard), and FUB knows where each contact came from.

Heads up: the FUB integration is included on Pro and Power plans. If you're on the entry tier, upgrade in Settings → Plan and the connection unlocks immediately — your existing API key keeps working, no re-paste required.

What syncs (and what doesn't)

We push the data Follow Up Boss can actually use, in the format it expects. No garbage fields, no forced custom-field mapping you have to maintain.

Pushed automatically on every enrichment

  • Owner first + last name. Real human, even when the property is owned by an LLC, Trust, or Corp — we unmask the entity-behind-the-entity.
  • Primary phone. Mobile preferred, landline as fallback.
  • Email address. Best available match.
  • Property address + custom fields. Years held, equity signal, list price (if known), property type.
  • Lead source = "LeadCove". So your FUB SmartLists can carve out leads that came from us vs. cold MLS exports vs. open-house sign-ins.
  • DNC and TCPA flags as tags. The compliance signal travels with the contact, so your action plan can branch — for example, skip auto-texts on DNC-flagged numbers.
  • Joint-owner data. When a property has two named owners (spouses, business partners), we send both contact records linked to the same address.

What we don't push

  • Raw vendor IDs. Internal-only. FUB stays clean.
  • Excluded leads. PO boxes, generic mail-forwarding addresses, government-owned parcels — those never make it to your dashboard, let alone to FUB.
  • Unenriched rows. If we couldn't deliver usable contact data on a row, it doesn't pollute your CRM with "Property Owner @ 123 Main St" placeholders. Garbage in, garbage out — we'd rather hold the row back than push junk.

The day-in-the-life

Here's what the integrated workflow actually looks like, end to end. This is what we see top producers running today.

7:30 AM · Morning intake

Pull today's list — expireds, withdrawals, FSBOs, building canvass, whatever. Drop the CSV into LeadCove. Hit enrich. Walk away.

7:45 AM · While you grab coffee

LeadCove enriches the list, scores each row A/B/C by equity and ownership length, and pushes every match to Follow Up Boss as a new person record. Each one tagged "LeadCove · YYYY-MM-DD" so it's easy to bulk-action later.

8:00 AM · Action plans fire

The new FUB contacts trip whatever action plan you've built — first text, first email, follow-up reminder. The plan respects the DNC + TCPA tags we sent, so you don't accidentally spam a litigator.

9:00 AM · Dial window

Open Follow Up Boss. Today's call list is already there: A-tier leads from this morning's intake, plus yesterday's no-answers cycling back, plus callbacks scheduled for today. You dial. The list doesn't have holes.

11:00 AM · Pipeline review

Conversation that went well? Move the FUB stage to "Listing appointment scheduled." LeadCove sees the FUB stage change via deep-link tag and surfaces those leads in your dashboard's Interested bucket too — so the prospecting view stays synced with the CRM view.

Why we don't replace FUB

Plenty of agents who try LeadCove already have years of muscle memory in Follow Up Boss. SmartLists they've built. Templates they've refined. Drip sequences they've tuned over hundreds of contacts. Throwing all of that away to migrate to a new CRM is a bad trade — even if the new CRM is great.

We built LeadCove to slot underneath FUB, not replace it. You keep every workflow you've already invested in. We just stop the bleeding on the one thing FUB doesn't do: turning addresses into deal-ready contacts at scale.

If you don't have a CRM yet, LeadCove also runs as a full prospecting CRM out of the box — Mission Control, lead manager, pipeline, archive, analytics. Same product, different starting point. But for the FUB users reading this: keep using FUB. We complement it, we don't compete with it.

The bottom line

Follow Up Boss manages relationships. LeadCove creates them. The five-minute integration between the two is the difference between a CRM that's full of stale leads from 2024 and one that has a fresh, enriched list hitting your morning dial window every single day.
